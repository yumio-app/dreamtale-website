Everything parents ask us

Your questions, answered.

What is DreamTale?

DreamTale is a bedtime story app where your child shares the spark — a character, a place, a silly idea — and you stay in charge. Every story is created just for your family, and nothing plays until a parent previews and approves it.

Is DreamTale a chatbot?

No. DreamTale is a guided story-creation flow with clear steps and choices — not an open-ended chat. Your child never types or talks back and forth with an AI.

What happens if my child records a voice idea?

The recording is used for one thing: turning their spoken idea into a story idea. DreamTale is designed to process the audio for that idea and not keep it longer than needed — what's saved is the idea, not the voice. Our Privacy Policy has the details.

Can parents review the story first?

Yes — always. Tonight's story appears on a preview screen first, and it won't play for your child until you approve it.

What safety controls are included?

Several layers working together: story guardrails, parent approval before playback, age-appropriate themes, guided inputs instead of open chat, and per-child limits that keep scary topics — or anything you'd rather skip — out of the story.

Can my child access stories more than once?

Yes. Approved stories are saved to your family library, so favorites can be replayed night after night — subject to your plan and app availability.

How many stories are included?

Every plan is designed to include 30 Bedtime Minis each month, a set of Premium Storybooks depending on your plan, and unlimited replays of the stories you've saved. Exact allowances may be fine-tuned before launch.

Is there a free tier?

We expect to offer a free starter experience so your family can try DreamTale before subscribing, plus early access for waitlist families. Final details will be confirmed at launch.

When will DreamTale launch?

Soon — and the waitlist is the front of the line. Joining it is the best way to get early access and hear launch news the moment it's ready.

Is DreamTale for bedtime only?

It's designed for bedtime — calm pacing, soft narration, sleepy endings — but families use stories whenever they want a gentle, screen-light moment: quiet time, car rides, rainy afternoons.
